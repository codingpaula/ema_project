The Eisenhower Matrix

What is the Eisenhower Matrix?

The Eisenhower Matrix is a time management tool originally invented by the former US president Dwight D. Eisenhower. When confronted with an overwhelming amount of tasks it can help you to set priorities based on the importance and urgency of your tasks.

And how does this work now?

The tasks added by you are put into the matrix based on their importance and due date. The matrix is divided into 4 fields that imply how the tasks in it should be handled:

  1. urgent and important - do now!
  2. not urgent and important - organize for when to do!
  3. urgent and not important - try to delegate!
  4. not urgent and not important - don't do (yet)!

How can I edit my tasks?

Simple click on the task's dot in the matrix. Then you can edit it in the pop-up modal window that appears.

I have a task that takes me three days to complete, so if i use the date it should be finished as a due date, the EMA will show it at the very front too late!

For very big tasks you have two options. Either split it up in smaller tasks that can be accomplished in one day or less or you see the due date that you fill in more as a latest starting due date and deliberately put it three days before the actual deadline.

Account and Settings

I have accidently ticked a task "done" and now it is gone!

Don't worry! Under "Account" - "Done Tasks" you can access a list of all your accomplished tasks and by clicking on a tasks name edit it.

Can I add a standard topic to be pre-selected when adding tasks?

Yes, you can. Go to "Account" - "Matrix", select the preferred topic and click "save".
